Halfmoon Cookies

Desserts with contrasting patterns of dark and light parts became popular at the beginning of the 19th century. Half-moon cookies, also called black-and-white cookies or half-and-half cookies, are frosted on one half with vanilla and one half with chocolate.

Half-moon cookies are made slightly different based on the region. New York City versions are flat with a dense cake base and a royal icing. Central New York and Boston versions tend to be dome shaped with a fluffy cake base and frosting. Some black-and-white versions use fondant instead of icing or frosting.

Cookie Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup shortening
  • 1 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup sour milk (1 tbsp white vinegar, add milk to make 1 cup)
  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Frosting Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup softened butter
  • 4 1/2 cups confectioner's sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 5-6 Tbsp milk
  • 3 Tbsp baking cocoa

Cookie Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 375F
  2. Cream shortening, sugar, eggs, and vanilla in a bowl.
  3. Add sour milk and dry ingredients. Mix well.
  4. Drop by 1/4 cup scoopful onto a greased baking sheet.
  5. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until edges and bottoms are beginning to brown.
  6. Let completely cool before frosting.

Frosting Directions

  1. In a large bowl, beat butter until creamy.
  2. Beat in confectioner's sugar, vanilla and enough milk to achieve desired consistency.
  3. Frost 1/2 of the bottoms of each cookie with the vanilla frosting
  4. Add baking cocoa to the remaining frosting. Mix well. May need a little more milk for desired consistency.
  5. Frost the other half of the cookie bottoms with the chocolate frosting.

Chocolate Chip Scones

As easy to make as chocolate chip cookies, these scones are a delicious treat with your morning coffee.

You can find many variations of this simple recipe using buttermilk, heavy cream, or butter. My favorite chocolate chip scones are made using butter. The texture is between a biscuit and a muffin. Light, flaky, and oh so tasty.

Using triple chocolate chips is heavenly. If you are feeling adventurous, you can add your favorite fruit to the scone dough too.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups all purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup white sugar
  • 3 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup cold butter (cut into small cubes)
  • 1 cup chocolate chips
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400F.
  2. Place flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl. Whisk them together.
  3. Add cold butter and cut it into the dry ingredients until crumbly consistency.
  4. Add the chocolate chips
  5. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg into the milk.
  6. Pour mixture into the dry ingredients.
  7. Mix together with your hands or a spatula until combined. Do not over-mix.
  8. Spread a bit of flour on flat work surface.
  9. Place batter on floured work surface and pat down into an 8 inch disk.
  10. Cut into 8 equal wedges.
  11. Place wedges on baking sheet.
  12. Bake for 16-18 minutes, or until just starting to brown on top.

These scones are delicious right out of the oven, or served at room temperature. If you don't want to bake all 8 scones at once, the dough can be frozen to bake at a later time. Just pop the frozen scones in the oven and bake for an extra 10 minutes.
